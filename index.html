Four Pillars of Innovation Funding Reform

Protecting Governor Shapiro's $100M Investment
Accountability provisions to maximize founder impact, ensure transparency, retain companies, and build a sustainable innovation economy
1
Direct-to-Founder Capital Mandate
The 60/40 Rule
Require that 60% of state innovation dollars reach founders as direct capital—loans, equity investments, or grants. The remaining 40% covers operating costs, technical assistance, and program delivery.
Why This Matters:
  • Current system: Only 25 cents on the dollar reaches founders (FY2025 data)
  • Under this mandate: $60M of the $100M reaches founders vs. $25M today
  • Enforcement: Shortfalls result in reduced appropriations the following year
  • Not punitive—it's a return to the original mission of supporting founders
2
Public Returns Transparency
Show Taxpayers the Scoreboard
If state dollars generate recycled capital through exits and repayments, publish the returns. Disclose Internal Rate of Return (IRR), fund performance, and exit outcomes so taxpayers can see whether their innovation investments are working.
What Gets Disclosed:
  • Fund IRR (gross and net) calculated annually
  • Multiple on Invested Capital (MOIC) for realized and unrealized investments
  • Total recycled capital generated through exits and loan repayments
  • Number of portfolio companies, exits, and jobs created
  • Protects proprietary information—only aggregate data disclosed
3
Regional Consolidation Study
Streamline for Efficiency
Direct DCED to evaluate Pennsylvania's four regional Ben Franklin Technology Partner centers to identify redundancies, measure overhead costs, and determine whether consolidation could increase the percentage of state dollars reaching founders.
Study Objectives:
  • Compare operational efficiency across four regional centers
  • Calculate administrative overhead as % of state appropriations
  • Identify overlapping services and duplicated infrastructure
  • Evaluate consolidation benefits while maintaining regional coverage
  • Not about eliminating presence—about maximizing efficiency
4
Build Retention Infrastructure
Keep Companies in Pennsylvania
Attracting founders means nothing if the first thing their coastal investors say is "have you considered relocating?" The ecosystem has to give people a reason to stay after they raise—not just a reason to start. That means follow-on capital, legal infrastructure, recruiting networks, and a community dense enough to matter.
Retention Requirements:
  • Multi-stage capital availability: seed through Series B within Pennsylvania
  • Legal & operational support: experienced startup counsel, HR infrastructure, compliance resources
  • Executive recruiting networks: proven ability to attract C-suite talent to Pennsylvania
  • Dense founder community: peer networks, knowledge sharing, mentorship at scale
  • Success metric: % of portfolio companies remaining headquartered in PA 3 years post-investment
